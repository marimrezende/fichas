Mask / Dirge
Mask
Conformist
You are a follower. It is easy for you to adapt, comply, and reconcile yourself to whatever new situation you find yourself in. You flit to the brightest star, throwing your lot in with the person you feel to be the best. It is both difficult and distasteful to go against the flow or rebel. You hate inconsistency and instability, and know that by supporting a strong leader, you help prevent chaos.
♦ Regain Willpower whenever your group (the brood) accomplishes something because of your support and aid.
✗ Never go against the flow or against the pack.
Dirge
Stoic
You take the unavoidable sorrows (and joys) of life in stride. You have a calm and serious mind, and rarely give into unseemly displays of emotion. This does not mean that you do not have feelings — you just do not wear them on your sleeve. The most people will ever get out of you is a slight smile or a small frown.
♦ Regain Willpower every time you come through an emotionally charged situation with your composure intact.
✗ Never let your emotions get the better of you.

House Bane
The Raven's Fracture
Morgana's mind is no longer linear.
Memory and perception fracture under pressure.
False Certainties
She remembers things with absolute confidence — that are wrong.
"Amanda told me to do this." — she didn't.
"This person is dangerous." — they're not.
"I've been here before." — she hasn't.
Reality Dropout
She blinks — three seconds gone. She hears something — it already happened. She's mid-action — she already finished.

The ST may open a scene with a Condition tied to the Bane — resolved by the character (losing time, exiting reality) or sent as false information contaminating her memory. The Condition renews on heavy Siris' Gambit use or after dramatic events.
Loki's Guise ••
Shapeshifting Bloodling Rank
Hidden Arsenal (Holdout)
Cost1 Blood per Size of object
Dice PoolNone
ActionInstant
The vampire can absorb an object into her person — to conceal it, protect it, or otherwise keep it within her body. She can absorb objects up to one Size category beneath hers, two items two Size categories beneath her, four items three Size categories beneath her, and an immense but reasonable number of smaller objects at Storyteller discretion.
Expelling an object is a reflexive action. It may come out of any part of her body. If forced into someone else, it counts as an attack: Stamina + Survival + Loki's Guise. Almost certainly grounds for a surprise attack if the victim didn't know about this power.
Chalice of the Overfull (Blood Bloat)
Cost1 Willpower
Dice PoolNo roll required
ActionReflexive
Allows the vampire to store more Blood Points in her body than it can normally hold. She must be at maximum capacity to activate it. Once active, she can hold a number of extra Blood Points equal to her score in Survival.
1 Bashing damage and −1 Speed for each two BP held above the standard maximum. These Health points cannot be healed while she exceeds her normal capacity.
Visually: veins and arteries bulge unnaturally. Mortal viewers will likely assume some hideous disfiguring disease — Storytellers may assess Social roll penalties accordingly.
Predatory Aspect
Cost1 Blood (activate) + 1 Blood per adaptation

DurationScene

Beast's Skin
Cost2 Blood
ActionInstant
DurationIndefinite
The vampire transforms into an animal she has previously consumed. The selected form must fall within Size 1–7. She can maintain a number of forms equal to her rank in Survival. In animal form, she adopts the beast's Physical Attributes and Skills, including its Size, Speed, and Health — she can move, sense, and attack in any way characteristic of the animal. She can still use her Gifts normally while transformed. She may be unable to use certain Physical Merits in this form.
To add or replace a form: consume the appropriate beast, then slumber for a full day and night merged with the earth.
Known forms: 🐕 Dog (Large) · 🦨 Weasel/Ferret · 🦇 Bat — see Animal Forms tab
Merge With Soil
Cost0 Blood (soil/earth) or Blood = Durability of material
ActionInstant
DurationIndefinite
The vampire sinks bodily into the earth for as long as she desires. Once interred, she is mostly insensible to the outside world, though she can remain conscious. She has a sense of the ground above her — she is aware if someone tries to concrete over her hiding place.
Vampire senses continue to function. Another vampire's predatory aura cannot force her into fight-or-flight while she is safely interred.
Blood absorption: if blood is spilled on the ground above her, she can absorb it. The soil dilutes Blood Point enough to prevent blood bond — but the taste might be enough to drive her out, thirsty for more.
Damage: 1 Bashing per point of Structure her haven loses. On the first point of Lethal damage, she emerges involuntarily.

Sanguine Thread
"A hair-thin psychic filament latches to a vein."
Cost1 Ichor
Dice PoolComposure + Larceny + Siris' Gambit vs. Stamina + Blood Potency
ActionInstant
Range10 m × Larceny
DurationScene
On success, immediately draw 1 Blood Point from the target into your pool (mortals take 1 Lethal instead). While the Thread endures, you may tug once per turn (Reflexive, no roll) to siphon 1 more BP (or 1 Lethal from mortals). The Thread breaks if the target leaves range or you dismiss it.
If the target is a vampire, you sense their current Blood Pool and whenever they spend blood this scene.
⚠ Raven Risk
If you tug more total Blood than your Larceny score in a scene through this power, you immediately suffer −2 to all Physical actions for the remainder of the scene (blood shimmer nausea).

Beast Speech
Cost—
ActionReflexive
DurationScene
Morgana communicates with animals of any kind — understanding their intentions, simple emotions, and basic thoughts, and conveying simple messages back. Communication is intuitive rather than linguistic: more sensation and image than words. Does not guarantee cooperation — only communication. Especially effective with animals she knows well (the horses at the stable, animals she has taken the form of).
Bird in Ear
Cost—
ActionInstant
DurationConcentration
The vampire pushes her consciousness into an animal she can see and perceives the world through its senses — sight, hearing, smell — as if she were the creature herself. While inhabiting the animal's perspective, her own body remains still and unresponsive. She can guide the animal toward targets of interest but cannot make it act against its nature.
Works especially well with birds and bats — silent scouts with high vantage points and keen senses.
Ending the link is reflexive. If the animal is killed or suffers serious harm while she is linked, she immediately snaps back to her own body and may be disoriented for a moment at ST discretion.
Cannot be used simultaneously with Beast's Skin — she cannot be inside an animal while also being one.

Combat Merits
Mobilize Outrage
Pre-Req: Brawl ••, Willpower 6 •••••
Your character has the hands of a revolutionary. She fights with the passion of the oppressed. She breaks skulls the way a slave breaks his bondage.

Bring Them Down
You always count your opponent's Defense as 1 lower than it is when attacking in melee. Also your attacks count as two attacks to penalize Defense.
2
Strike to Preempt
When spending Willpower on an attack, add her Willpower dots to the attack roll instead of the normal +3. She loses her Defense.
3
Unbreakable
When resisting Mental or Social coercion — including Disciplines — spending Willpower adds her Willpower dots to a contested roll, or subtracts from the opponent's roll on a resisted roll (instead of the normal +3 or −2). Only usable when actively engaged in combat, or initiating combat next turn. May spend a second Willpower in the same turn to activate Unbreakable after activating Strike to Preempt.
4
Dying On Your Feet
Remains standing and fighting long after most vampires would fall. While in combat: no wound penalties, and not subject to torpor when Health boxes are full of lethal damage. Only aggravated damage — and Final Death — may stop her. At the end of the combat scene, she falls into torpor if her Health boxes are still full of lethal.
5
Destroy Defense
Each successful hit reduces the target's Defense by 1, cumulatively across successive attacks — up to a maximum equal to the lower of Morgana's Wits or Dexterity. Usable across multiple rounds as long as she does nothing except deliver successful attacks. Resets to 0 on a miss or any other action. Cumulative with the normal Defense penalty against multiple attacks.

Flaws
Striking Looks
•• · 2-Point Flaw
Your character is ugly, alarming, or otherwise worthy of negative attention — extensive burn scarring, a glass eye, a face that draws stares. For two dots, lose 2 dice on any Social rolls influenced by looks (Expression, Intimidation, Persuasion, Subterfuge, and others at ST discretion).
Any rolls to spot, notice, or remember her gain a +2 dice bonus. She may draw unwanted attention in social situations, causing further complications.
Chronic Pain (Mild)
• · 1-Point Flaw
An injury or disorder leaves her in severe pain on a regular basis. Roll 4 dice once per day. On failure, a bout of pain begins — timing at ST discretion, usually during waking hours or triggered by stress.
While in pain: −2 to all skill rolls · +1 success required on frenzy rolls.
Pain usually lasts one hour; roll Stamina + Survival each hour to recover. If the attack occurs during sleep, suffer sleep deprivation penalties instead and fail to recover the nightly Willpower point.
No Depth Perception
• · 1-Point Flaw
Lacks effective binocular vision and cannot visually judge distances. May have one eye, two eyes that don't work together, or a glass eye.
−1 Dexterity in combat and on any task involving hand-eye coordination · −3 on ranged attacks (unless Aim action taken first) · −3 on rolls to operate any vehicle faster than a horse
Unlucky
• · 1-Point Flaw
Spectacularly unfortunate. Disaster follows in her wake. A number of times per day equal to the points in this Flaw, the Storyteller may force a reroll of a successful roll and take the second result.
